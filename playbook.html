Meetings & async

The playbook.

Our working agreements, made practical. Copy-paste templates for the things we do every week — so good meetings and clear async communication become the easy default, not extra work.

Meeting agenda Meeting notes Decision doc Async update Handoff

Before you book a meeting

Ask: could this be a message? If a written update or thread would do, write it instead.

No agenda, no meeting

If there's nothing to decide or align on, there's nothing to meet about. Send the agenda ahead of time.

Default to writing

Decisions and updates go in a place others can find later — especially teammates in other timezones.

Close the loop

Every meeting ends with decisions, owners, and next steps written down and shared.

Meeting agenda

Async update / standup

Post in your team channel instead of a live standup. Quick to write, easy to scan, and it works no matter what hours people keep.

End-of-day handoff

When work needs to continue across timezones, leave a clear handoff so the next person can pick it up without waiting for you to come online.

🤝 Handoff — [your name] → [next person / team] · [date]

- [Link to the doc / branch / ticket]

NOTES
[Context the next person needs — gotchas, decisions made, who to ask.]

Adapt these freely.

These are starting points, not mandates — trim what you don't need. They put our working agreements into practice, and they work best alongside each person's preferences on the roster (someone who reflects before speaking will value that pre-read; someone in a far timezone will lean on the handoff).
